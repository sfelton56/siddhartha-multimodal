Siddhartha

Flowing, the river called out to Siddhartha. His name, once held in high regard, meant nothing to the churning waters at his feet. He imagined the world and all of it’s Gods laughing at what he had become. Siddhartha the child person, once Siddhartha the samana, was lost. His soul, empty of meaning, yearning to be understood, was far from enlightenment.


The river was tantalizing. He longed to succumb to its depths—never to resurface again. Never to face the disappointment that he now felt. He wished to flow as it flowed, to become whole again. With a deep breath, he took one last look at the world and readied himself for death.


As he stepped into the water, something deep within his soul began to stir. After years of wandering without purpose, the samana in him longed for peace—not death. After years of sleepwalking, the river had awoken his soul.
